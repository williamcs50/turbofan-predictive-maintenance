# v0.3 Simulator Redesign: Design Notes

## Target SNR

### What SNR target you picked

Each affected channel reaches SNR = 4 at RUL = 0, where SNR = degradation delta / sensor noise std. The trajectory within the label window (RUL ≤ 30) depends on each mode's physical dynamics and is specified per mode below: gradual ramp, quadratic ramp, or abrupt step.

### Why

4 sits above the rough ~2× detectability threshold, but below the regime where any linear model trivially succeeds. The ramp is intentional: weak signal early, strong signal near failure. The ramp justifies the SNR target; the multi-sensor signatures justify the architecture.

### What you considered and rejected

Keeping the current magnitudes (SNR ≈ 1) was ruled out. The May 27 diagnostic confirmed anomaly and normal windows are statistically indistinguishable, and no loss function recovers from that.

Targeting SNR ≥ 10 was also ruled out. If logistic regression scores perfectly on raw features, the result says nothing about whether the Transformer is doing useful work.

## Multi-sensor signatures

### Mode 1: High-pressure turbine wear

- **Vibration** rises: mechanical wear causes blade imbalance and tip rub as clearance opens.
- **T50** rises: a worn turbine extracts energy less efficiently, so the engine burns more fuel to hold thrust and exhaust gas runs hotter.
- **P30** falls: the HPT drives the HPC on the same shaft; a weaker turbine delivers less shaft power, so the compressor can't pressurize as hard and discharge pressure sags.
- **Timing:** vibration leads (mechanical symptoms appear early and subtly), T50 and P30 follow as efficiency degrades toward end-of-life.
- **Magnitude:** each affected channel (vibration, T50, P30) reaches SNR = 4 at RUL = 0.

### Mode 2: Compressor Stall

- **P30 falls with growing oscillation**: Airflow through the compressor separates from the blades (stall condition), causing periodic detachment and reattachment. This creates large, unstable pressure fluctuations at the compressor discharge.
- **T50 rises sharply**: Disrupted airflow leads to inefficient combustion and reduced compressor efficiency. The control system burns extra fuel to maintain thrust, driving exhaust gas temperature higher.
- **Vibration increases**: Aerodynamic instability and unsteady flow generate strong structural vibration.
- **Timing:** Abrupt event signature (~10 cycles of warning). Sits well inside the RUL ≤ 30 label window.
- **Magnitude:** SNR ≈ 0 from RUL = 30 down to ~RUL = 10, then a step to SNR ≈ 4 at event onset. All three channels move together.

### Mode 3: Main Bearing Failure

- **Vibration rises quadratically**: Progressive loss of lubrication and increasing clearance leads to metal-on-metal contact, imbalance, and severe mechanical vibration.
- **T50 and P30 show negligible change**: The heat generated is highly localized to the bearing housing and does not propagate meaningfully to the compressor discharge pressure or downstream exhaust gas temperature sensors.
- **Timing:** Physical onset ~40 cycles before failure, but the RUL ≤ 30 label window opens midway through the ramp. The early portion (RUL 40→30) is labeled normal. Faint early degradation that isn't yet anomalous is a realistic property, not a mislabel. The labeled portion (RUL 30→0) spans the steeper half of the quadratic, where SNR ramps from ~1 to 4.
- **Magnitude:** vibration channel reaches SNR = 4 at RUL = 0.

### Mode 4: Foreign Object Damage (FOD)

- **Vibration spikes abruptly**: Sudden ingestion of debris damages blades or creates immediate rotor imbalance, producing a large step increase in vibration.
- **T50 and P30 remain largely flat**: No gradual thermodynamic degradation occurs; any secondary efficiency loss is minor compared to the dominant mechanical shock.
- **Timing:** Abrupt event signature (~10 cycles of warning). Sits well inside the RUL ≤ 30 label window.
- **Magnitude:** SNR ≈ 0 from RUL = 30 down to ~RUL = 10, then a step to SNR ≈ 4 at event onset. Vibration channel only.

**Note on abrupt modes (stall + FOD):** 20 of the 30 labeled anomaly cycles will have sensors that look completely normal. Signal only appears in the last ~10. This is not a labeling bug; it's a realistic property of abrupt failures where there is genuinely no warning 30 cycles out. Diagnostic plots will show flat traces under an anomaly label for these modes. Expect it.

### Mode 5: Overheating

- **T50 rises steeply (linear ramp)**: Progressive degradation of cooling flows, hot-section fouling, or control-system drift causes sustained elevation of exhaust gas temperature.
- **P30 and vibration remain essentially unchanged**: This is a pure thermal signature with no significant aerodynamic or mechanical component driving the other sensors.
- **Timing:** Physical onset ~60 cycles before failure. The RUL ≤ 30 label window captures the latter half of a linear ramp. The early portion (RUL 60→30) is labeled normal — faint early rise that isn't yet anomalous.
- **Magnitude:** Linear ramp means SNR ≈ 2 at the label window boundary (RUL = 30), rising to SNR = 4 at RUL = 0. T50 channel only.

## Next iteration

The current design gives every affected channel SNR = 4 at end-of-life. A future refinement is distributed SNR: primary sensors hit 4, secondary (correlated) sensors hit 2–3. This would make the cross-sensor pattern harder for a linear model to exploit and more clearly justify the Transformer's multi-head attention over sensor combinations. Deferred to v0.3.x after learnability baseline confirms the current design is learnable.
